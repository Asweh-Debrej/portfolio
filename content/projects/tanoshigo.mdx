# TanoshiGo

## Problem

JLPT study resources are fragmented: dictionaries here, drill apps there,
flashcard tools elsewhere. None of them combine high-quality study with the
social, competitive loop that keeps people coming back day after day.

## What I built

A single platform with two modes:

- **Study mode** — a ~7.1k-word JMdict-backed dictionary with SM-2 spaced
  repetition, five quiz types covering N5–N1, and a sibling-sense deduplication
  fix so words with multiple meanings don't double-count in your queue.
- **Play mode** — real-time versus matches across seven gamemodes
  (`SPEED_QUIZ`, `KANA_BLITZ`, `VOCAB_SHOWDOWN`, `READING_SPRINT`,
  `SENTENCE_BUILDER`, `PARTICLE_WAR`, `WEEKLY_TOURNAMENT`) with an ELO ranked
  ladder (Bronze → Apex 頂点) and seasonal MMR resets.

An admin dashboard (TipTap + Recharts + cmdk) lets me edit content, monitor
match health, and triage user reports without leaving the browser.

## Outcomes

- ~3 months of focused development to closed beta (Mar 2026).
- 15 alpha testers actively playing through Study + Play loops.
- Live at [tanoshigo.com](https://tanoshigo.com) on Dokploy across GCP +
  DigitalOcean VPS, with CI/CD on every push.

## Tech highlights

- **SM-2 sibling-sense dedup** so polysemous words schedule one review, not five.
- **2-D weighted question pool** (`match_type × tier_level`) keeps every versus
  match within a player's skill band while still varying question shape.
- **kuromoji** token-level scoring drives Reading Sprint — partial credit by
  morpheme rather than whole-string matching.
- 30-game **calibration phase** before placement, then seasonal ranked tiers
  named after Japanese folklore.
- Redis-backed match state + MinIO for user-generated audio.
