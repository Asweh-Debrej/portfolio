# Elephant Robot — KRAI 2023

## Problem

KRAI 2023 ("Casting Flowers over ANGKOR WAT") asked teams to build an
autonomous robot that navigates a competition field and accurately throws
objects within strict time limits — all without GPS, on a resource-constrained
microcontroller, with reliable master-slave coordination between two robots.

## What I built

Tech lead for the **elephant robot** division at Unit Robotika ITB (URO):

- **Motor driver + PID** (`Motor.cpp`, `pidLo`) tuned for smooth low-RPM
  response and predictable acceleration profiles.
- **3-encoder odometry** (`odometriKRAI`) for indoor localization where GPS
  isn't an option.
- **Path tracking** with **Stanley Pursuit** for high-speed stability, plus a
  **Sliding Mode Control** variant tested for disturbance-robust runs.
- **Sensor fusion** combining TFMini ToF and CMPS12 (IMU/compass) for
  position correction.
- `JoystickPS3` manual override for tuning and emergency stops.
- Also contributed to `Master_Rabbit` for the companion rabbit robot.

## Outcomes

- **National finalist**, KRAI 2023, with the URO ITB team.
- Hands-on engineering experience under real-time embedded constraints and
  competition pressure (a humbling tutorial in latency).

## Tech highlights

- **Fixed-point math** for 3-encoder odometry on STM32 to keep the control
  loop real-time without an FPU.
- **Stanley Pursuit** chosen over Pure Pursuit for stability at high speed.
- **Sliding Mode Control** evaluated as a disturbance-robust alternative for
  uneven competition surfaces.
- **Complementary filter** (TFMini + CMPS12) lighter than Kalman and good
  enough for correction without over-compute.
