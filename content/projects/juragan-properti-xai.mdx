# Juragan Properti, Multi-Source Housing Market XAI

## Problem

Indonesian property data is fragmented across listing sites with inconsistent
schemas, and the few price-prediction models in academic literature are
black-box regressors that don't explain *why* a price is what it is. We wanted
both: a unified dataset and an interpretable model.

## What I built

- **Scraping pipeline**, Selenium-driven crawler against
  [99.co.id](https://99.co.id) with header rotation and headless retries to
  collect listings across Indonesian metropolitan areas.
- **EDA + feature engineering**, location clusters, neighborhood density,
  derived ratios (price/m², land/building ratio), and segment tagging
  (low/mid/high) since price drivers differ per segment.
- **Modeling**, XGBoost and Random Forest baselines plus PyTorch MLPs,
  evaluated per-segment so the high-end tail does not distort low-segment fit.
- **XAI layer**, SHAP for global and per-listing feature attribution.
- **FastAPI backend** serving model predictions and SHAP explanations to a
  Next.js + Mapbox GL frontend with heat-map visualization.

## Outcomes

- Published as **"Integrating Multi-Source Data and Explainable AI for Housing
  Market Analysis in Indonesia"** in the
  [International Journal of Housing Markets and Analysis](https://doi.org/10.1108/IJHMA-01-2026-0006)
  (Emerald), March 2026.
- Co-authored with Ivan Aldy Ganesen, Fawwaz Abrial Saffa, Ayu Purwarianti,
  and Nugraha Priya Utama.

## Tech highlights

- **Per-segment models** beat the single-model baseline because property price
  drivers behave differently across low/mid/high tiers.
- **SHAP** turned the model from a black box into a usable analyst tool.
- **Mapbox GL heat map** for spatial exploration of predicted vs actual.
- **Selenium with rotating headers + headless retries** to survive anti-bot
  measures on the source site.
