# Robotic Arm Control & Visualization

## Problem

A Mechanical Engineering lab at ITB needed a friendly tool for practicum
sessions: students should be able to drive a robotic arm and see joint
angles update in real time without dropping into firmware code.

## What I built

I contributed the **web interface, visualization, and FE↔firmware
communication** layer:

- Full UI with Next.js + HeroUI for two arm configurations (`arm1`, `arm2`).
- Konva.js **real-time kinematics visualization** of joint angles (theta, omega)
  with smooth 60fps updates.
- **WebSocket** channel between the browser and ESP32 firmware.
- **Forward-kinematics** math in JavaScript so the visualization runs locally
  without round-tripping to the device.
- Consulted on firmware architecture for serial communication and stepper
  motor control.

## Outcomes

- Actively used during practicum sessions at the ITB Mechanical Engineering
  lab.

## Tech highlights

- **Konva scene graph** with hierarchical joint transforms, child joints
  follow parent rotation naturally.
- **Smooth 60fps multi-segment arm rendering** even at extreme poses.
- **NaN handling** for trigonometric edge cases at the singularity of arm
  reach so the UI never goes blank.
